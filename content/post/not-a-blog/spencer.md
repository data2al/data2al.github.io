As the chill wind swept through the night, the trees rattled with the rhythmic tapping of the cold rain outside. I sat at my desk, brooding in the darkness, waiting for the title "Spencer" to appear on my computer screen. "Spencer" is a film that showcases an exaggerated version of Princess Diana's Christmas dinner with the royal family, where she confronts her husband about his infidelity.

"As Diana sits at the dinner table, she feels she would faint any moment now. She steals an angry glance at Charles, who stares back blankly, a sense of curiousness in his eyes. It was as if he were at a chess table, waiting for her to make her next move. She slowly turned her gaze away. Staring across the table, she looked sadly at the ghost of Anne Boleyn, wondering if she'd be beheaded like her, who was put in the same situation by King Henry VIII. As she pondered over the idea of death, the pearls around her neck started to tighten up, as if they were a noose..." 

"Spencer" outlines the heartache and complexities of romantic relationships nurtured under the shade of the Royal Family. Royals enjoy a great many luxuries. Yet freedom and true love seem to be something they cannot afford.
