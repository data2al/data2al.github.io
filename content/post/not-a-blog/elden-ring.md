"As I woke at the Site of Lost Grace, I looked across the half-abandoned battlefield in the barren valley below me. I could see Knights and Soldiers strolling through the area. Their stiff movements betray their state of fatigue. 

Like a madman, I charged into the nearest group of soldiers, hoping to eliminate any early threats before crossing the field. Halberd in one hand, Sacred Seal in the other, I circled the group and rounded up my prey, strafing and poking, trying to thin out the herd. The soldiers fought back valiantly, yet their attacks fell short as I rolled and dodged out of harm's way. As another group of Soldiers closed in, I waved the seal I was holding, and a burst of flame errupted from my hands, engulfing them, melting armor and burning flesh. Without looking back, I ran quickly into the valley, fearing more enemies would come..."

Such was the experience of 'Elden Ring', a game designed to challenge its players with complex terrain and interesting battles. In 'Elden Ring', you start out as a lowly Tarnished, an exiled member of the Round Table that once served the Queen. Your mission is to roam the 'Lands In-Between' and uncover your findings about the all-powerful Elden Ring. The 'Lands In-Between' is a beautiful island, filled with castles and towers that belonged to various nobelity. Monsters are the player's least concern, as fearsome Dragons soar through the skies while skilled horsemen gallop over the grasslands. A heavily guarded mages' academy stands firmly on top of a tall rock, located in the middle of a misty lake. A mysterious stone Manor sits on the edge of a volcano, marred by hot flames, yet filled with maze-like dungeons and chilling secrets. 

For those of you who wish to learn more, "KiteTales & Flex" has a youtube series explaining the lore of 'Elden Ring'. I highly recommend it.
