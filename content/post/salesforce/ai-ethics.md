### Types of Bias
#### Measurement or Dataset Bias
Can be introduced when a person makes a mistake labeling the data or through machine error. A characteristic or factor can be over-or-underrepresented in your dataset.

#### Type 1 vs Type 2 Error
Type 1 - false positive, predicts positive result but is actually negative;
Type 2 - false negative, predicts negative result but is actually positive;

#### Association Bias
Bias labels based on stereotypes, it results in association bias.

#### Confirmation Bias
Bias labels based on preconceived ideas, such as recommendatoins reflecting your purchase habits. 

#### Automation Bias
It imposes a system's value on others due to the lack of variety and limits of the training data.

#### Societal Bias
Prejudice toward historically marginalized groups.

#### Survival Bias
An algorithm focused on results that were selected or who survived a certain process at the expense of those who were excluded.

#### Interaction Bias
Human interaction with AI influencing the results it creates, such as teaching chatbots bad language.

### How to remove Bias
#### 1. Conduct Premortems;
#### 2. Identify Excluded or Overrepresented Factors in Your Dataset;
What: Statistical patterns that apply to majority may be invalid within a minority group;
How: Consider creating different algorithms for different groups rather than one size fits all.
What: People are excluded from your dataset, making it impossible to see the effects in the data; 
How: Look for what researchers call unknown unknowns, errors that happen when a model is highly confident about a prediction that is actually wrong.
#### 3. Regularly Evaluate Your Training Data;
Address Data quality issues as early as possible, address extremes, duplicates, outliers and redundancy in the data preparation tools.
