## Main Ingredients of AI

1. Yes-and-No Predictions
2. Numeric Predictions
3. Classifications
4. Recommendations

---

**Yes-and-No Predictions** generally comes in the form of a probability. These probabilities are sometimes converted into scores, such as on a numeric scale of 0 to 100.

**Numeric Predictions** often power predictive forecasting, such as "how much revenue", "how many days". 

**Classifications** frequently use "deep learning" capabilities to operate on unstructured data like free text or images. Another type that may or may not use deep learning is "clustering". This method finds common features within the dataset and groups datapoints based on those features. 

**Recommendations** are key when you hae a large set of items that you'd like to recommend to users. Based on what the users have bought in the past, AI is able to recommend new products to the customer. In addition, Marketers use the same technique to recommend content like whitepaper to business users. HR use it to recommend job postings to candidates. 

---

### Workflow and Rules
They are technically not part of AI, but are essential to its usage. There usually are follow up actions after assessment or predictions are made that helps improve the business. For instance, if a customer has a 25% likelihood of not renewing their contract, a workflow should kick in to help retain this customer, like an email or promo campaign of some sort.
